# Speaking at Nordic Summit 2026: Microsoft Copilot Studio vs Azure AI Foundry

Here's a question that comes up constantly in Power Platform projects right now.

You've built something with Microsoft Copilot Studio. It works. Users are happy. Then the requirements shift — the knowledge base grows, the orchestration gets complex, the model needs tuning — and suddenly you're bumping into the edges of what Copilot Studio can do cleanly.

Do you push through with Copilot Studio? Move to Azure AI Foundry? Build a hybrid?

Most teams don't answer that question upfront. They answer it late, under pressure, after they've already invested months in one direction.

---

I'm speaking at **Nordic Summit 2026** on exactly this topic.

The session is **Microsoft Copilot Studio vs Azure AI Foundry** — a practical decision framework and architecture playbook for Power Platform developers and solution architects who need to make this call in real enterprise projects.

Nordic Summit is the largest in-person Microsoft Power Platform and Dynamics 365 conference in the Nordics. The 2026 edition runs **21–22 September** at LEGOLAND® Hotel & Conference in Billund, Denmark.

The session covers:

- When Copilot Studio's low-code speed is the right call
- When Azure AI Foundry's pro-code control is non-negotiable
- How to architect hybrid solutions — Copilot Studio as the front door, Azure AI Foundry as the engine room
- Real-world patterns from Dataverse, Power Platform, and enterprise agentic scenarios

The framing I keep coming back to is the **hybrid architecture pattern**. It's where a lot of real enterprise deployments end up — not by design, but because teams outgrow Copilot Studio on one dimension while still needing its low-code speed and Power Platform integration on another. The session is built around making that trajectory visible before you're already committed to the wrong side of it.
